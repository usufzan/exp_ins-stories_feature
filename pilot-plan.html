Pilot plan

Proof experiment plan

The next step should be a short proof experiment, not a long vendor approval. The experiment should tell us whether the experience is measurable, controllable, and useful.

Pass/fail gates for StorifyMe

Controlled rollout: We can decide exactly who sees the stories.
Clean holdout: a comparable group can remain unexposed.
Event tracking: impression, open, completion, skip, dismiss, replay, and CTA click can be tracked.
Joinable analytics: events can include story ID, cohort ID, pseudonymous user ID, timestamp, and placement.
CTA routing: CTAs can deep-link into the relevant insurance flows.
Content workflow: marketing can create and update stories without engineering help.
Privacy check: no unnecessary PII is sent to the vendor.
Written scale pricing: pricing is clear for 5k, 25k, 50k, and 100k targeted users.

What to measure

Engagement

  • Impressions
  • Opens
  • Completion rate
  • Skip and replay rate
  • CTA click-through rate

Learning

  • “Was this helpful?”
  • “I understand my insurance options better”
  • Qualitative feedback
  • Reduction in repeated confusion

Business behavior

  • Insurance switching
  • Cancellation intent
  • Drop-off in insurance flows
  • CTA conversion into relevant actions

Operations

  • Time to create a story
  • Time to update content
  • Engineering requests per update
  • Marketing independence

Questions to ask vendors

Ask StorifyMe

  • Can we show the widget only to internally flagged users?
  • Is the embed iframe-based or native SDK/DOM-based?
  • Which events can be exported or forwarded?
  • Which analytics features are included in each tier?
  • What data does the SDK collect by default?
  • Can we run on monthly pilot terms?

Ask Storyly

  • What is the shortest possible PoC?
  • Which tier includes targeting, A/B testing, event export, and third-party analytics?
  • Can user-level attribution use a pseudonymous ID?
  • Can Storyly offer pilot terms without annual commitment?
  • What does Storyly do that Expatrio cannot do with internal targeting plus a lighter vendor?

Recommended technical setup

Build one wrapper component

The vendor should sit inside one Expatrio-owned component: <StoryRail />. This wrapper controls placement, eligibility, cohort, holdout, events, and CTA routing. That keeps the vendor replaceable.

If StorifyMe fails the proof-experiment gates: move to Storyly under a short PoC, not a long-term contract.
