Vendor details

What each vendor is good for

The short version: StorifyMe is the best pilot fit, Storyly is the strongest platform, and the open-source options are build-path references rather than pilot vendors.

Primary

StorifyMe

Use for: fast proof sprint. Good balance of setup speed, CMS, web/app support, and analytics.

  • Strong enough to validate the idea.
  • Lower friction than enterprise platforms.
  • Main risk: advanced segmentation and analytics may require a higher plan.
Open demo →
Backup

Storyly

Use for: advanced targeting, analytics, A/B testing, and scale.

  • Probably the strongest product overall.
  • Better for a later scale phase.
  • Main risk: higher cost and more lock-in.
Spike

StorySDK

Use for: fallback, build-path learning, and a small technical spike.

  • Open SDK plus hosted editor.
  • Useful for understanding the internal-build option.
  • Main risk: small vendor and unconfirmed targeting/GDPR/A-B.
Open demo →

Vendors to park for now

InAppStory

Strong on paper, especially for mobile engagement. But pricing is quote-only, onboarding is sales-led, and web dashboard fit needs confirmation.

react-instastories

A good React renderer if Expatrio builds later. Not a vendor because it does not include CMS, hosting, targeting, or analytics.

zuck.js

A vanilla JS story renderer. Useful only as a build reference. Weaker fit than react-instastories for a React dashboard.

Storyly, later

Storyly should stay in the conversation. It may be the right choice after the pilot if the format works and advanced vendor-side capabilities matter.
